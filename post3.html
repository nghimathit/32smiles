Brushing Toddlers: How can I help my 2-year-old learn to brush?

- Proper oral hygiene is an important habit from the moment the first tooth erupts, but it can be difficult by the time your child is 2 years old because they don't like brushing and flossing at this time. faculty a bit. The secret to successful brushing is to make this routine a fun experience.
Parents are children's habits:

-Toddlers often like to imitate the actions of their parents. Use that to brush your teeth with your child. When you see you brushing your own teeth, your child will find this habit quite interesting, and that's when you set a good role model for him. Stand in front of a mirror with your child so he can see and imitate your actions. Use funny facial expressions when brushing your teeth to make this routine more enjoyable for your child.

Sing a song:

When there's music, everything around becomes happier; The same goes for brushing teeth with toddlers. Just like singing a song that teaches your child to clean up his toys, when brushing his teeth, choose a song that is related to keeping your child in a happy mood when cleaning their teeth. Find a song about brushing your teeth to sing with your child, or simply make up a fun song yourself. Make creating a fun atmosphere a regular part of your routine, including singing before you start as a sign that it's time to brush your teeth, or you can also sing while brushing your teeth. This routine becomes light and fun.

Brush your teeth with friends:

-Make brushing your child's teeth a fun activity by bringing in their favorite stuffed animal. Teach your child to brush his teddy bear's teeth first and then brush his own teeth. Another fun idea is to let your child brush your teeth first, and then you will brush your baby's teeth.

Funny brush:

A toothbrush in your child's favorite color or decorated with a familiar character will make brushing more fun. Another great way is to give your child the power to choose. Children are adamant about not brushing their teeth simply because they want to be able to make their own decisions. As long as you don't mention not taking care of their teeth, instead, let your child choose how they want to brush their teeth. For example, you could offer two types of toothbrushes and let your baby choose which one she will use that day.
Try these simple tips to make the brushing routine more enjoyable for your child. These tips won't work for all babies, but stick with them until you find the best method. Remember that even if your child has established a good oral care routine, you still need to supervise them, at least until they are 6 years old and can brush their own teeth properly.
