What Is Dry Mouth?

-Dry mouth is a condition in which you do not have enough saliva to keep the oral cavity moist. We all experience dry mouth, especially when we're worried, sad, or stressed. But if you have persistent dry mouth, it can be uncomfortable and can lead to more serious health problems or signal an underlying health problem in your body. The reason is because saliva not only moisturizes the oral cavity, but also helps digest food, protects teeth from decay, prevents infection by controlling bacteria, and effectively supports chewing activities. and swallow.
How to Detect Dry Mouth?

Occasionally, we feel dry mouth. If this condition persists, your salivary glands may be in trouble. Symptoms of dry mouth include:
-Sticky, dry feeling in the mouth
-Difficulty in swallowing
-Tongue is burning
-Dry throat
-Chapped lips
-Difficulty in taste or metallic taste in the mouth
-Mouth sores
-Frequent bad breath
-Difficulty chewing/speaking

How To Treat Dry Mouth?

The only long-term cure for dry mouth is to treat its cause. If your dry mouth is caused by medication, ask your doctor to change your prescription or dose. If your salivary glands aren't working properly but still produce a small amount of saliva, your doctor may prescribe a medication to help your salivary glands work better.
If the cause of dry mouth cannot be eliminated, or while waiting for radical treatment, you can restore moisture to the oral cavity in a variety of ways. Your dentist will advise you to choose a moisturizer for the mouth area, which has the effect of replacing saliva. You can rinse your mouth with a specially formulated mouthwash to help you feel more comfortable. At the same time, you can:
-Regularly sip water or sugar-free drinks
-Stay away from caffeinated beverages, like coffee, tea, and some sodas that can dry out your mouth
-Chew sugar-free gum or suck on sugar-free hard candy to stimulate saliva production (if the salivary glands are still functioning)
-Do not use tobacco or alcohol as they cause dry mouth
-Be careful: spicy or salty foods can make you feel pain when you have dry mouth
-Use a humidifier at night
