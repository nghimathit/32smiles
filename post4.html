What is tooth decay?

-Tooth decay is a painful disease for patients caused by bacteria. Let's find out with Colgate what tooth decay is and find out the causes as well as how to treat and prevent tooth decay below.
What is tooth decay?

Tooth decay is an infection caused by Streptococcus mutans bacteria, which destroys minerals of inorganic and organic components, damages hard tissues and causes small holes in the teeth.
Among them, the types of tooth decay must include:
- Tooth decay. The most common type of tooth decay, occurring in both children and adults, crown caries usually occurs on the chewing surfaces or between teeth.
- Tooth decay. As we age, our gums are no longer strong, causing part of the root of the tooth to be exposed. Since there is no enamel covering, these exposed root areas are susceptible to tooth decay.
- Secondary caries. Formed around the areas of your teeth that are filled with fillings and crowns. This condition occurs because these areas often tend to accumulate plaque.
Tooth decay is a very dangerous disease. If left untreated, tooth decay can destroy your tooth and damage all the tiny nerves in the central area of the tooth. This can lead to a tooth abscess, an infection at the tip of the tooth. Once an abscess forms, you can only be treated with a root canal, surgery, or extraction.
Any tooth can have decay, in which, the most common are still molars and incisors.

What causes tooth decay?

When you eat foods that contain carbohydrates (sugars and starches), these carbohydrates are eaten by bacteria that stick to plaque, which in turn produces acids that damage teeth. Over time, enamel begins to break down below the tooth surface while the tooth surface remains intact. When the enamel layer below the surface is eroded, the tooth surface collapses, forming a cavity

Causes of tooth decay:

-Do not clean the plaque.
-Dental defects.
-A diet high in carbohydrates and sugar promotes the growth of plaque-forming bacteria.
-The tooth surface is more susceptible to caries when it is poorly calcified, less exposed to fluoride and/or in an acidic environment. Typically, calcium loss begins when the pH on the teeth drops below 5.5 (for example, when lactic acid-producing bacteria multiply or use soft drinks, sports drinks, or energy drinks, which often have pH below 5.5).
-Dry mouth can be caused by disease, medication side effects, radiation and chemotherapy, and can be temporary (days to months) or permanent, depending on the cause. mouth.
- Genetic factors.

Symptoms and signs of tooth decay:

Although often you will feel certain pain stemming from tooth decay. But other visual and sensory factors can also help you determine if tooth decay has formed. According to the Mayo Medical Center, common symptoms of tooth decay include:
-The change in color on teeth. It can be black, brown or white.
-Feeling discomfort such as sharp, aching or dull pain.
-Pain when biting or eating food.
-Teeth with large holes, tiny holes on the teeth, although there is no pain or discoloration.
-Sensitivity, sensitivity to hot/cold temperatures
Are these signs of tooth decay? However, it is a sign that the tooth has been severely decayed. Only your dentist can tell for sure if you have tooth decay. That's because cavities develop below the surface of the teeth, where you can't see them.
Cavities are more likely to develop in holes in the chewing surfaces of molars, in between teeth, and near the gum line. No matter where cavities form, the best way to detect and treat them before they become serious is to visit your dentist for regular checkups.

When to see a doctor to diagnose and treat tooth decay?

Tooth decay can be repaired by prosthetic methods, performed by dentists.
-Remineralization method to harden enamel with Calcium or -Flour, usually applied to the early stages of tooth decay.
- Method of filling teeth. This method is applied to teeth that are decayed but have not reached the pulp or tooth nerve. At this point, the dentist will remove the cavity and fill the tooth with a large cavity with composite or amalgam.
- Method of taking root canal or taking root canal. When the decay has spread to the pulp or nerves, you must perform this method.
+ Myelography. When the dental nerve is mildly infected, there is still the ability to recover naturally.
+ Take the tooth pulp. The dentist will determine the extent of the nerve damage and remove the pulp if the tooth has moderate or severe damage or infection.
- Method of teeth reduction. If the tooth is severely damaged by decay, it may need to be extracted.

How to prevent tooth decay effectively:

Tooth decay can turn your life upside down. Therefore, if the tooth has not been decayed, prevent it now, by the following ways:
-Brush at least twice a day and floss daily to remove plaque between teeth and below the gum line.
-Visit your dentist regularly. Proper oral care can help prevent dental problems and keep them from developing into more serious problems.
-You can better support tooth enamel against decay by using a fluoride toothpaste and a fluoride treatment prescribed by your dentist. Use an ADA-approved fluoride toothpaste such as Colgate Total® to ensure the best results in strengthening tooth enamel.
-Build a balanced diet, limiting starchy or sugary foods. When you eat these foods, try to eat them with your daily meals instead of snacks to minimize the number of times your teeth are exposed to acid.
-Use a fluoride oral care product such as a fluoride toothpaste.
